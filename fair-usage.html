What counts as fair use

Examples of normal, expected use:

  • Uploading a backlog of historical health documents when you first set up the App
  • Adding new test results or letters as you receive them
  • Photographing supplement or medication products
  • Periodically generating AI health summaries

What counts as excessive use

Examples that may trigger rate limiting:

  • Uploading large volumes of documents in a short period inconsistent with personal use
  • Rapidly and repeatedly requesting AI processing in quick succession
  • Using automated tools, scripts, or bots to interact with the App
  • Uploading documents unrelated to your personal health records to consume AI capacity

We do not publish specific numeric thresholds to retain flexibility to respond to abuse patterns. This policy is intended to address abusive or automated use patterns and is not designed to affect ordinary personal health record management.

Rate limiting

We reserve the right to apply temporary rate limits at any time without prior notice where usage is excessive or inconsistent with personal health record management. Rate limiting is a temporary measure and does not constitute a breach of our Terms of Service. Refunds are not provided where rate limiting results from your excessive use.

Consequences

If usage is determined to be excessive, we may: (1) apply temporary rate limiting, (2) contact you to discuss the pattern, (3) suspend or terminate access if excessive use continues after notice. We will always apply the least restrictive measure appropriate.
